# Regulatory Knowledge
_Last updated: 2026-05-24_

Ontario commercial carrier compliance — the full regulatory domain covered by ExeSketch and CVORReady.

## CVOR — Commercial Vehicle Operators Registration

The CVOR certificate is the operating licence for Ontario commercial carriers. Every carrier operating a vehicle with a registered gross weight over 4,500 kg requires one. The certificate must be carried in every vehicle.

**CVOR scoring formula:**
- Points assigned for collisions, convictions, and out-of-service orders
- Collisions and convictions are double-weighted vs inspections
- Each carrier has a threshold based on fleet size and kilometres driven
- Violation rate = (points accumulated / threshold) × 100%

**Safety rating thresholds:**
- Excellent: violation rate ≤ 20% overall AND ≤ 15% collision
- Satisfactory: violation rate ≤ 100% (audited or unaudited)
- Conditional: violation rate > 100% OR failed facility audit
- Unsatisfactory: severe violations, typically leads to suspension

**Conditional rating — upgrade path:**
- Must maintain ≤ 60% of overall CVOR threshold for minimum 6 months
- Must pass a facility audit
- Contact: cvor@ontario.ca
- Cannot upgrade through on-road performance alone — audit required

## MTO Facility Audit

The MTO conducts facility audits of carriers, typically triggered when violation rate exceeds 80% of threshold or specific profiles trigger concern.

**Three audit profiles:**
1. Driver / Operator Qualifications (DQ profile)
   - Excellent ≥ 80%, Pass ≥ 55%, Fail < 55%
   - Common violations: incomplete driver files, missing abstracts, no annual reviews
2. Vehicle Maintenance (VM profile)
   - Excellent ≥ 80%, Pass ≥ 55%, Fail < 55%
   - Common violations: incomplete maintenance records, no PMVI documentation, defect records missing
3. Carrier Safety and Practices (CS profile)
   - Excellent ≥ 80%, Pass ≥ 55%, Fail < 55%
   - Common violations: no collision investigation files, no preventability determinations, missing safety meeting records

**Fail-any-profile rule:** A carrier fails the facility audit if ANY single profile scores below 55%, regardless of performance in the other two profiles.

**Post-audit outcomes:** Exit interview with CEO/senior official, charges may be laid, conviction points on safety rating, Conditional rating if failed, must pass re-audit to upgrade.

## NSC Standards (National Safety Code)

Canada-wide standards for commercial carrier operations. Ontario enforces these through the MTO and O. Reg. 555/06 (HOS) and O. Reg. 199/07 (daily inspection).

**NSC Standard 11 — Preventive Maintenance**
Carriers must establish and follow a preventive maintenance program. Minimum content: PM schedule per vehicle, maintenance records, annual inspection records, defect and repair records, out-of-service and return-to-service records.

**NSC Standard 13 — Driver Training**
Requirements for driver training and orientation. New drivers must receive documented orientation before solo operation.

**NSC Standard 14 — Load Securement**
All cargo must be secured to prevent movement. Key rules:
- Tiedowns must have aggregate WLL (Working Load Limit) ≥ 50% of cargo weight
- Tiedown count formula: one for cargo ≤ 1.52m, two for 1.52m to 3.04m, add one per additional 3.04m
- Re-inspection required within 80 km of loading and after any change
- Cargo-specific rules: coils (chocked or cradle), lumber (stacked and tied), pipes (nested or bulkheaded), aggregate (enclosed or covered)
- Edge protection required where tiedowns contact sharp edges

**NSC Standard 15 — Safety Management**
The primary audit standard. Carriers must demonstrate a functioning safety management system including: written safety policies, driver qualification files, vehicle maintenance program, hours of service compliance, collision investigation program, safety meetings and records.

## Hours of Service (HOS)

**Ontario domestic — O. Reg. 555/06:**
- 13-hour driving window within a 16-hour on-duty period
- 70 hours maximum in any 7 consecutive days
- 10 hours mandatory off-duty after each shift

**Short-haul exemption — O. Reg. 555/06 s.76:**
- No log book required if driver returns to home terminal within 160km
- Driver must be released within 14 hours of reporting to work
- Cannot be used more than 5 days in any 7-day period

**Cross-border — US FMCSA 49 CFR Part 395:**
- 11-hour driving limit within 14-hour window
- 70 hours in 8 days (or 60 in 7 days)
- 10-hour mandatory off-duty
- ELD required unless exempt
- Carry-forward rules apply when crossing from Canadian cycle

## Incident Reporting Obligations

**$5,000 threshold (updated January 2025):**
Any collision involving property damage over $5,000 or personal injury is reportable. Previously $2,000 — updated January 2025.

**Three carrier obligations on reportable collision:**
1. Report to police immediately (if not already at scene)
2. Report to MOL within 24 hours if critical injury: 1-877-202-0008
3. Complete written report to MTO within 48 hours of critical injury

**Scene preservation:** If a worker is critically injured, the scene must be preserved and no equipment moved until MOL inspector attends or authorizes. Penalty for disturbing scene: up to $2,000,000.

**Collision file contents (required for CVOR defence):**
- CR-CS-005: Collision Investigation Report
- CR-CS-006: Driver Incident Statement (within 24 hours, driver's own words)
- CR-CS-007: Preventability Determination Worksheet (within 5 business days of police report)
- Police Motor Vehicle Accident Report
- Photos, dashcam footage, telematics data
- Pre-trip inspection for that day
- HOS records for that day

**Zero-pointing a collision:** A documented non-preventable determination supported by evidence (clean pre-trip, no HOS violation, no driver impropriety, dashcam footage) results in zero CVOR points for that collision. Without CR-CS-007 on file, the carrier cannot make this argument at audit.

**Driver conviction self-reporting:** Driver must report any Criminal Code or HTA conviction to carrier within 15 days. Carrier must update CVOR record.

**Near-miss:** Not legally required to report but evidence of proactive safety culture at MTO audit. CR-CS-008 Near-Miss Report supports Excellent rating argument.

## Insurance Requirements

**Ontario domestic minimum:** $2,000,000 public liability and property damage. Mandatory for CVOR certificate.

**US cross-border (FMCSA):**
- $750,000 USD minimum public liability (most shippers require $1,000,000)
- BOC-3 filing required — process agent in every US state of operation
- Cargo insurance typically required by shippers: $100,000-$250,000

**Owner-operator integration:**
- WSIB coverage — carrier must file Form 1149A (Certificate of Independent Operator) per vehicle, per owner-operator
- VIN-based determination: if the owner-operator does not own the vehicle they drive, they may be deemed an employee regardless of contract
- Driver Inc. risk: CRA increasingly treats owner-operators paid through corporations as employees if carrier controls work conditions — carrier faces back taxes and penalties

## Dangerous Goods (TDG)

Carriers transporting dangerous goods under Transport Canada's Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act (TDGA) must:
- Train all drivers handling DG (CR-TDG-001: training record)
- Carry proper safety marks on vehicle and container (CR-TDG-002)
- Have ERAP (Emergency Response Assistance Plan) reference for Class 1, 6.1, certain Class 2 and 3 (CR-TDG-003)
- Complete TDG shipping document for each shipment (CR-TDG-004)

DG training is a driver qualification file requirement — CR-DQ-008 must be on file.
